About Me

Angela Tan De Asas was born in December 1999 in Catubig, a small town in Northern Samar. Her uncle, her mother's only brother, gave her the name Angela. She completed her elementary and high school education at a public school in her hometown. For her senior high school years, Angela moved to a more bustling town, Catarman, to attend the University of Eastern Philippines (UEP).

Upon graduating from UEP, Angela enrolled in San Beda University in Manila, where she initially pursued a degree in Accountancy. Her family supported this choice, believing it would be an excellent foundation for a future career in law and a way to carry on the family business. However, Angela soon realized that she didn't feel a sense of belonging there. Feeling like an outcast without genuine friendships, she decided to shift her path. Angela then transferred to the University of the East, where she is now pursuing a degree in Computer Engineering, embracing a new direction that better aligns with her true passions and goals.

As the youngest child for 15 years, with a 5-year gap to her elder sister and a 10-year gap to her elder brother, Angela often found herself alone. This solitude nurtured her diverse passions and hobbies. Angela developed love for visual arts, experimenting with various mediums to express her creativity. She also enjoyed playing sports like badminton and archery. Her interests extended to tinkering with broken electronics, trying out new recipes in the kitchen to satisfy her culinary cravings, and gardening via planting medicinal herbs and vegetables.

In her quieter moments, Angela found solace in watching sunsets and stargazing, often reflecting on the world around her. Sometimes, she shares these peaceful experiences with friends, adding a social dimension to her contemplative pursuits.
